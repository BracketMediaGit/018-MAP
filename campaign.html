The Fight

This isn't a drug policy debate. It's a homicide policy debate.

Fentanyl kills faster than almost any other substance in modern history. A pill crushed into a line. A capsule slipped into a night out. Someone's son. Someone's daughter.

The people who supply these doses know exactly what they're moving. And in too many states, when a young person dies from that dose, the dealer faces a drug charge — not a homicide charge.

MAPDA exists to change that, everywhere.

The Five-Front Plan

Five fights. All winnable.

A coordinated campaign built for moms who pick fights — and win them.

Front 01

Win Colorado. Then pick one more.

Join Advance Colorado as a named coalition partner on the ballot initiative. Target one stalled state bill — Connecticut, Virginia, or Hawaii — for a MAPDA-led push.

Front 02

Own the federal bill.

Launch the MAPDA 50 — one mother from every state, photographed on the Capitol steps — behind the Felony Murder for Deadly Fentanyl Distribution Act.

Front 03

Stand the Courtroom Corps.

Bereaved families in matching shirts at every drug-induced homicide trial and sentencing. Prosecutors, judges, juries, and press all notice.

Front 04

Publish the prosecutor scorecard.

Rank every DA by whether they charge fentanyl deaths as homicide. Endorse in races. Primary the refusers. Statutes become real only when prosecutors use them.

Front 05

Write the model bill.

Ship the MAPDA Model Fentanyl Homicide Act — with Good Samaritan carve-outs that neutralize the harm-reduction critique and make laws durable against rollback.
